What is TTML IMSC1?

TTML IMSC1 (Timed Text Markup Language Internet Media Subtitles and Captions 1.0) is a specification developed by the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C). It defines a standardized profile of the broader TTML1 and TTML2 specifications, specifically tailored for delivering subtitles and captions over the internet.

Its primary goal is to ensure global interoperability and consistent rendering of timed text across various platforms, devices, and regions. IMSC1 enables subtitles and captions to be presented with precise timing, positioning, and styling, making audiovisual content more accessible to diverse audiences, including individuals who are deaf or hard of hearing, as well as those watching in different languages or noisy environments.

IMSC1 simplifies the complexity of the full TTML language by narrowing it down to a core set of features most essential for web-based video captioning. By doing this, it eliminates unnecessary variation and improves compatibility between streaming services, media players, and authoring tools. It supports both text-based and image-based captions, providing flexibility depending on the content requirements.
