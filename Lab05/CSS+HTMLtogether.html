Best Design Practices | Yap (Lab05)

When we were tasked with making our html page look pretty all I could think about was what colors to use for them. Automatically I thought, I should do it like how I did lab04 and didn't think to hard about it and because of that mindset I was running with, I missed an incredibly vital tool. JAVASCRIPT making the 3 seperate htmls wasn't too hard because when I was done with one I copied and pasted the rest which made things simpliar than I thought. But, it began to become tedious and thats when I knew something was wrong but I didn't want to work on it anymore for that day. So I wanted to work on the visually aspect of the webpage. All I knew was I wanted the colors to be Purple, Orange, and Blue, but I also wanted the webpage to be appealing and functional but, little did I know it wasn't going to be easy. In lab04 we just had to slap some colors together as practice to make a webpage somewhat appealing. This time around I actually wanted the webpage to look appeal for myself and others. The color scheme was the easiest part, but having some sort of sense for design is something I know I have but implementing is difficult. I needed to know about some principles like contrast and readability. Initially, the color scheme was decided on. But figuring out how to balance orange, blue, and purple without making the site look like a chaotic mix of well... chaos. Thatâ€™s when I turned to AI, asking for guidance on how to implement three distinct themes while keeping them easy on the eyes.

With the help of AI, I learned how to structure Flexbox for the orange theme,Grid for the blue theme, and a minimalist purple theme using clean and simple design elements,like the banner/bar behind the references that you can click to. That was by far the best implementation to the whole webpage. It gave it way more definition. Imagine it like drawing a ball, you can draw and color the ball and it might look alright, but adding shadow below it just gives it so much more definition. The goal was to make each theme feel unique but still keep everything looking simple, professional, and modern.Orange had to feel warm and energetic, but not overwhelming (was good enough for me). Blue needed to give off a serious look/vibe. guy in a blue suit with a suitcase type of vibe . Purple, this by far is my second favorite as it just looks nice, and No I dont have an artsy way of conveying what I was going for just wanted it to look nice. The text always being readable was the second most important aspect of the design process.

By the end, what started as a simple idea and a bare bone page ðŸ’€ became a good looking webpage. The relief I felt when I saw the webpages were switching themes as they were supposed to brought so much joy to me.
